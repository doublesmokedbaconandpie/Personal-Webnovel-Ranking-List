Amane Fujimiyas neighbour in the apartment he lives in is the schools number one lovable angel: Excellent and with a level of beauty that can only be described as angelic Mahiru Shiina. Amane, an ordinary student who doesnt stand out, is her neighbour, but until now, they didnt interact with each other. After meeting the soggy angel in the rain:

Im returning what I borrowed. By the way, clean up your room. Its filthy.

None of your business.

A relationship with a sharp-tongued angel begins, starting with handing over an umbrella. From catching a cold and having someone take care of you, to making a meal for someone because they neglected their health, to joint work (cleaning rooms)

Get your insulin shots ready because you're gonna die from extreme diabetes after reading this novel. This novel really tugs at your heartstrings and leaves a warm feeling in your chest when reading it. Its free from the useless arguments, quarrels and misunderstanding that others of the same genre has, instead it just focuses on the slow and steady development of our two main characters. Its one of those few novels that could make your heart go doki doki and ache all at the same time.

The story is just about... more>> a gloomy boy and an angelic girl, and their daily interactions with each other. Their relationship gets closer and closer with each passing chapter. And you as a reader just can't help but grin from ear to ear as their feelings for each other slowly develops from being friends to something more.

Our two main characters have quite the contradicting personalities which leads to some interesting interactions and conversations. Both of them are fleshed out, likeable and just plain cute and fluffy. Each one of them has their own flaws and problems which they try to improve as the story progresses. Their daily interactions with each other are wholesome as f*ck and there are some moments that leaves you foaming at the mouth from the cuteness overload.

There are not many side characters in this novel and that's not really a problem. Each one of the small cast of side characters are memorable, lovable and just mesh well with our main characters.

The translation is barely passable. There are some spelling errors here and there, occasional hard to read sentences, misuse of pronouns and an overuse of the word "wry". Even though there are some problems with the translation, it doesn't really take away the experience from reading the novel.

Highly recommend for everyone to read. It's a heartwarming slice of life light novel that just leaves you wanting for more. Its warm like drinking hot cocoa on a cold night and as wholesome as a pack of Quaker's oatmeal. Its a diamond in a rough that everyone should give a try. <<less

Contradictory as it may be, despite me liking this novel, I can't wholeheartedly recommend it

The setup of Angel Next Door is pure and simple: she was a girl, he was a boy, cannot make it any more obvious. What you'll see is the standard romance progression where the girl clearly liked the boy, the boy is too dense, and their friends get exasperated about it. Sure, just because it's standard doesn't mean it's not good, but it does mean there's not much that can be said about it.

The problem with... more>> sugary sweet romance novels is that, for some reason, post relationship is an incredibly hard thing to write. Once relationship gets established, tension simply disappears and things get dry real fast, despite the fact that the couple kept doing the sweet thing that got you hooked in the first place. For 3 entire volumes Amane and Mahiru do sweet things together, and then it goes downhill

This is not a problem that only Angel Next Door experiences, OmiaiShitanode also suffered from the same problem, although that one swung way too hard in the other direction. This is not to say that the series as a whole is bad, but there's a peak where the series clearly is great, only to go downhill and it's really not your fault if you suddenly find yourself thinking "Hmmm this isn't as enjoyable as previous volumes"

For Angel Next Door the peak is, sadly enough, volume 3. Author claimed that volume 4 is where the hero "gets proactive". No spoilers but the volume is pretty disappointing. The one amusing part of the later volumes is the fact that Mahiru grows younger every year: she starts out a 16 year old, by volume 5 she's basically 12

But enough of doom and gloom! If Volume 4 sucks, what about volume 1-3?
Well, dear reader I'm glad you asked because volume 1-3 are incredible. Like I said it's very basic, but it's really sweet and in a good way. There's always something new in their relationship and the standard "oh come the hell on why aren't you guys dating yet?" interactions are limited. Angel Next Door inspired a helluva lot of other romance slice of life novels and you can see why: the setup really works

Although, basic and wish fulfilling that it is, you may not find it that enjoyable. That's fine, I also don't think this is a literary masterpiece, but if you find yourself liking volume 1, do grab 2 and 3, it'd be worth it <<less

This will be review for both WN and LN.

To explain briefly: this story is about fluffy romance between two main characters Amane and Mahiru. No harem and no other BS, only pure romance. If you're tired of reading harem novels or if you're looking for one-on-one pure relationship, this novel is for you.

The light hearted comedy and fluffy moments are nice; the back stories of Mahiru and Amane are really touching and quite believable and so is their reason for falling with each other. Their relationship is definitely diabetes inducing.... more>> However the story suffers from a major drawback: repetition.

The story takes an extreme nose-dive after the confession and the two characters practically do nothing but repeat their earlier lifestyle and actions. They soon realize after becoming a couple that they were already doing couple like things even before dating each other. Sadly, even when the characters realize this themselves, it is the author that doesn't understand this and repeats the same sort of actions and the story then heads towards nothing but fluffy action and no plot or development of any sort.

To quote another reviewer, this is basically rinse and repeat of pre-defined and over used sequence of actions between the two characters. Mahiru pouts and Amane tries to lift her mood. And then some fluffy action happens. Rinse. Repeat. <<less
